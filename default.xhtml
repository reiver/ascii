ASCII

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

ASCII (short for American Standard Code for Information Interchange) is a historically important character-set that people billions of people have used either on its own or embedded in other character-sets such as CP437 (code page 437), Unicode, and others.

It affected what symbols were shown on many computer-keyboards. It affected what symbols were shown on many computer-screens. It affected what symbols were formed the basis of many Internet computer-protocols. And more.

128 Characters

ASCII has 128 characters — numbered 0 to 127.

Although many (probably most) people who have used ASCII have used it as part of character-sets with more-than 128 characters (such as CP437 (code page 437) and Unicode), ASCII itself has only 128 characters.
